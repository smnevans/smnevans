Tron: Legacy

Jan 11th, 2011

I went to ‘Tron: Legacy’ with a pretty clear idea of what I was getting into. I expected the visuals to be fantastic, and the story to be very average.

It surpassed my expectations in both regards. The visuals were even more beautiful than I’d hoped. The story and dialogue were much, much worse than I’d feared. So much so that I wish I’d gone to see the dubbed Japanese version.

No, actually, I’d still be able to understand too much of the dialogue. If you want to get the most enjoyment out of ‘Tron: Legacy’ see it dubbed in a language you don’t understand at all. I imagine it would be quite good in French.

How to Park a Car

Dec 21st, 2010



  1. Park the car.
  2. Kill the lights.
  3. Engage the handbrake.
  4. Get out of the car.
  5. Lock the door.
  6. Immediately forget that you’ve locked the door.
  7. Walk into the supermarket, department store, or wherever.
  8. Realize that you don’t remember locking the door.
  9. Walk back to the car.
  10. See that, as usual, the door is locked.
  11. Repeat, four times a week for the rest of your life, because there was that one time eight years ago that you came back to check the lock and it actually was unlocked, and weren’t you lucky you caught that?

One time.
Eight years ago.

Painting the Bathroom

Dec 16th, 2010

After four months of procrastination I finally painted the bathroom today and somehow did it without killing myself.

It was a little hairy at times, I had to stand on tiptoes on the rim of the bathtub to reach the ceiling above. I almost lost my balance a couple of times but my ninja training kept me on my feet.

Timewise, it was a big improvement over my last painting effort. That took three days to finish and, truth be told, still isn’t really done. There are little bits of masking tape I’ve not yet removed. The bathroom today only took about six hours to get everything finished and cleaned up.

Wait Up, Baldy!

Oct 31st, 2010

So, I’m coming out of a convenience store this morning. Coming towards the store is a father with his two kids, a boy and a girl, both around seven or eight. Very normal looking family for these parts, dressed in Sunday casual. Tracksuits.

The girl, who is on a bike, is going a little bit fast and crashes into another bike. Her purse and cell phone fly from the basket to the ground. “Pick it up!” she barks, to her father, who does as he’s been told. No hint of anger or annoyance.

As she untangles her bike, her brother and dad head into the store and she shouts “Matte, hage!” A polite translation would be “Wait up, baldy!” Again, no reaction from dad. He keeps walking toward the store. She screams this three more times, increasingly loudly.

Wait up, baldy!
Wait up, baldy!
WAIT UP, BALDY!

A light rain is beginning to fall so I head home to my daughters. I guess they’re not so bad after all.
