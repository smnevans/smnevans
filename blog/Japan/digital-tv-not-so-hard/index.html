Digital TV: Not So Hard After All

Sep 18th, 2011

On July 24, when Japanese TV stations stopped analog broadcasts, I’d done nothing to get our TV system digital-ready. I’m not the biggest TV fan. The prospect of not having it for a while was about as daunting as imagining life without SPAM. I reckoned I’d have to pay for a tuner, an antenna, and for someone to come out and install it. I was sure it would be expensive, time consuming and end up with me feeling somehow ripped off.

We were in Australia at the end of July so it wasn’t an immediate problem. And even when we got back at the end of the month it was fine. If the kids wanted to watch something really badly they went to their grandmother’s house and watched it there. I knew it was something I’d have to take care of someday, though. On a Sunday near the end of August I took a deep breath and decided to do what needed doing.

Less than an hour later we were up and running. It didn’t cost a thing.

The first happy surprise was finding out that our TV, though rather old, was already digital-ready. That meant I wouldn’t have to get a tuner.

I headed out to the balcony to take a look at the antenna and wiring. I was somewhat incredulous when I saw that there was actually a UHF antenna perched on top of the VHF one.

Where did that come from? I know I didn’t buy it. It must’ve been sitting there unused and unloved since we bought the house twelve years ago.

Twenty minutes later, after messing about with cables and TV settings a bit, we were good to go. All the noisy variety shows, irksome melodramas, and screechy singing programs you could ever hope to watch were back for our viewing pleasure in vibrant high-definition.
